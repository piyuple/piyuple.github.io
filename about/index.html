About

My name is Piyush and I’m a Machine Learning Engineer.

I love to understand how things actually work, not just using them, but really getting them. Mathematics isn’t just a tool for me; it’s a lens for seeing patterns in everything from machine learning to music to the way assembly code flows.

This blog is where I think out loud about the things I’m working on and learning. If something here clicks with you, reach out.

Mathematics & Computation Link to heading

There’s this moment when you realize numbers aren’t just a line. They’re a plane. Negative numbers aren’t just “backwards” but we can rotate into entirely different dimensions. The complex plane turned my understanding of mathematics sideways (literally), and Euler’s identity captures why I find this so compelling:

$$e^{\pi i} + 1 = 0$$

Five fundamental constants in one elegant equation.

This same mindset carries into my work. I don’t just want to know that a neural network converges. I want to understand the gradient flow. I don’t just tokenize text for an LLM. I think about the linguistic structures being carved up and reassembled.

Building Things Link to heading

Data Science: I work with PyTorch, building models and actually understanding the mathematics underneath. The “why” matters as much as the “how”.

Game Development: This is where math becomes art. Physics simulations, shader programming, sprite design, procedural audio. It’s a creative playground that happens to be built on linear algebra and signal processing.

Solving Puzzles Link to heading

My brain likes patterns. Algorithmic problems, sudoku, strategy games like chess and backgammon. These scratch the same itch. There’s something satisfying about finding the elegant solution hiding in complexity.

Outside the Terminal Link to heading

I run. Long distances, usually on weekends with friends. There’s something meditative about it. The rhythm, the endurance, the mental clarity that comes around kilometre 16.

I read fiction when I can. Dostoevsky’s psychological depth, Herbert’s world-building, Heller’s absurdism, Liu Cixin’s cosmic perspective. I’m drawn to stories that make me think differently.

What Drives Me Link to heading

Curiosity. The desire to automate the tedious so there’s more time for the interesting. The belief that understanding fundamentals unlocks creativity at higher levels.

I like when disciplines overlap. When mathematics illuminates music, when game design teaches you about human psychology, when a long run clears your head enough to solve a coding problem you’ve been stuck on.
